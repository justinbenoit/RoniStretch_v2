Roni Stretch, born into a working class family just outside of Liverpool, England, began exploring the world of art at the age of five. The understanding of perspective and the attention to detail were so unusual, especially for someone so young, that Roni’s drawings were taken to a child psychologist to be analyzed. His school`s preoccupation with his ability eventually gave him a complex about his artistic talent so he put down his paint brushes, and stepped out of the spotlight.

Some twenty years later, Roni found himself back in the limelight when he was spotted by Elite Model Agency in London. Soon he began traveling the globe, being photographed for many the world’s top designers, modeling taught him many things with regard to the human figure: a way of seeing modes of movement, light and shadow interplaying, and the unforgiving eye of the camera as it records all. These experiences combined with the contemporary scenes of fashion, sports, and music served as an inspiration and subject matter for many of his future paintings.

A little over ten years ago Roni settled in California, through the encouragement of a fashion editor Roni renewed his passion for art. He rediscovered the voice of his five year old self. Today, Roni`s work combines the formal aspects of the Abstract Expressionists with his own brand of sensitivity, charm and wit. Represented in New York by the Steve Michel gallery and recently appointed by both the Peoples Choice and the American Music Awards as their official artist and by the U.N. to produce a canvas monument to celebrate each of the fifty states of America, Roni is fast becoming an international art figure.

Roni has chosen football as the inspiration for his latest series of works.An exhibition of these works will be on display in london spring 2002, followed by a global tour of these and other works.

" In a world where our lives are saturated with images from the obsure to the every day, art seems needlessly to struggle while trying to cross boundaries. The action and ideal of my work is to convert the stigma associated with abstraction by choosing themes with which every body can relate. With the universal game of football, I truly hope my images will extend the normal parameters and increase the interest in art ."




Q - What inspired you to start painting?
A - A painting on a wall in an apartment my friend was staying at triggered this whole journey. I felt I had painted it.

Q – Which painters do you admire most?
A – Many different, for different reasons, constantly changes, today I’d say, Avery, Rothko, De Kooning, Heron, Diebenkorn, Valdes, Turner.

Q – What has the high point been in your career so far?
A – In such a short career to date, I seem to have had many. When the first serious collector viewed my work and left my studio with some 22 pieces later, remarking as he left "now go and paint!"

Q – What inspired your recent works?
A – Diebenkorn’s Ocean Park series really enabled me to view my compositions in such a geometric way that bridged my gap from realism to abstraction. By leaving all my evidence in the development of the work also really freed me up.Today, I’m very interested not solely in the subject matter but the process.

Q – How would you spend your last ten bucks?
A – Beach parking $5, cup of coffee $3.50, probably give the change away

Q – Describe the Roni Stretch masterplan.
A – Keep working, try to create a great painting ,still working on that, give back as much as I can

Q – What was the last risk you took?
A – Just painting

Q – What can you see from your bedroom window?
A – Most of my house. It’s all glass

Q – Which artist living or dead would you most like to collaborate with?
A – Tough one. Probably Modigliani.

Q – What’s your idea of the perfect colour?
A – Nature

Q – Sex, drugs or Rock N Roll – Which would you choose if you had to?
A – Have to be good old sex

Q – What is the thing you most/ least like about yourself?
A – Perfectionist/ Perfectionist

Roni Stretch grew up in the North of England. After a successful career modeling with Elite which took him all over the world, Roni eventually settled down in Los Angeles. Middle of 2 brothers, Conrad elder, Gary younger, Gary was the former British and World International Champion Boxer. His Dad is a plumber. Roni Stretch has now been living in the USA for over ten years. He still has his Liverpool St Helens accent…
